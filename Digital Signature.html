Digital Signature Certificate


What is digital signature certificate?

Digital Signature Certificate established the identity of the sender for filing or sending documents electronically through the internet. Similar to a handwritten Signature that establishes the signee's identity and consent, DSC establishes the identity and consent for electronic records.

All government departments in India accept digitally signed documents from CCA like eMudhra, Panta Sign, V-sign, etc.


How do I get a digital signature certificate?

We can help you obtain Class 3 Digital Signatures with a validity of 2 or 3 years. There is no requirement to submit the documents manually or through courier. We can help you obtain Class 3 signatures from a certifying authority like E mudhra, Panta Sign, V-sign.

All the Digital signatures are provided in FIPS compliant ePass USB token to protect the Signature until the end of the validity.


Documents Required for Indian Nationals


The following documents must be submitted by the Indian nationals while applying for Digital Signature Certificate..

✅ Identity Proof
✅ Passport
✅ PAN card of the applicant
✅ Driving License
✅ Post office ID card
✅ Any photo issued by the Government which bears the Signature of the individual
✅ Address Proof
✅ Aadhar Card
✅ Voter ID
✅ Driving License(DL) Registration Certificate(RC)


Documents for Foreign Nationals


In case the applicant is a Foreign National, he has to submit the following documents for identity and address proof:

✅ Identity Proof
✅ Attested copy of
✅ Applicant passport
✅ Attested copy of VISA (If the applicant is out of the native country)
✅ Attested copy of Resident Permit Certificate (If the applicant is in India)
✅ Address Proof
✅ Attested copy of the applicant’s passport.
✅ Attested copy of any other government-issued address proof.


The following authorities must attest the identity and address proof of foreign nationals:

Embassy of the Native Country (if the applicant is out of the native country) Apostilized by Native Country, after public Notary(if the country is in Hague Convention) Consularized by Native Country, after Public Notary (if the country is not Hague Convention)
